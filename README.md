## Architecture Mindset

I approach every system with a set of first-principles that define production-ready engineering:

**Scalability** — Systems are designed horizontally from the ground up, with stateless services, distributed caching, and workload-aware autoscaling on Kubernetes.

**Observability** — Every pipeline exposes structured logs, distributed traces, and semantic metrics. Incidents surface themselves before they impact users.

**Resiliency** — Failure is a design input, not an afterthought. Dead-letter queues, circuit breakers, idempotent consumers, and graceful degradation are standard patterns.

**Event-Driven Architecture** — Decoupled, asynchronous communication enables modular systems that evolve independently without coordination overhead.

**Distributed Systems Thinking** — Consistency boundaries, partition tolerance, and eventual consistency are explicitly reasoned through at the design phase — not discovered in production.
